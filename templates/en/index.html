Did you know?

  • The Mona Lisa has no eyebrows—it was the fashion in Renaissance Florence to shave them off!
  • Van Gogh only sold one painting during his lifetime and yet, he painted over 900 works.
  • The longest time it took to complete a painting was 18 years for Jan van Eyck's Ghent Altarpiece.
  • Did you know? Picasso could draw before he could walk and had his first exhibition at the age of 13.
  • Salvador Dalí would often get out of paying for drinks and meals by drawing on the checks, making them priceless.

Fine Art Reproduction

An Art Replica is a contemporary reproduction of a famous painting from the past.

It is the result of artisanal work, entirely executed by hand by a professional painter.

It is a work of art made with oil paints on aged canvases or panels, crafted in accordance with the original methods and techniques. It is the outcome of serious and meticulous research and preparation that goes into the finished painting.

A Fine Art Reproduction allows for the enjoyment and familiarity of what is typically the exclusive heritage of museums or a select few.
